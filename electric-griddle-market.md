<p><p>The electric griddle market consists of two primary types: manual control and thermostatic control. Manual control griddles allow users to set temperature settings manually, requiring close attention for optimal cooking. In contrast, thermostatic control griddles feature built-in temperature regulators that automatically adjust heat, ensuring consistent cooking temperatures without constant monitoring. These differences cater to various consumer preferences, with manual control appealing to those who enjoy hands-on cooking, while thermostatic control suits those seeking convenience and precision in their cooking experience.</p></p>
