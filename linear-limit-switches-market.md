<p><p>The linear limit switches market is primarily divided into two types: electromechanical switches and solid-state switches. Electromechanical switches use moving parts to operate, providing reliable performance but may wear over time. They are ideal for conditions where durability is essential. In contrast, solid-state switches employ electronic components to detect limits without mechanical movement, offering longer life and faster response times. These switches are suitable for applications requiring high reliability and reduced maintenance, like in automation and robotics.</p></p>
