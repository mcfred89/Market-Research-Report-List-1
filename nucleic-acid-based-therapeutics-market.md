<p><strong>The Nucleic Acid-based Therapeutics Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Anti-Sense And Anti-Gene</li><li>Short Inhibitory Sequences</li><li>Gene Transfer Therapy</li><li>Nucleoside Analogs</li><li>Ribozymes</li><li>Aptamers</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The nucleic acid-based therapeutics market includes several types, each targeting specific processes within cells. Anti-sense and anti-gene therapies utilize complementary nucleic acid sequences to inhibit gene expression. Short inhibitory sequences, such as siRNAs, silence target genes. Gene transfer therapy involves delivering therapeutic genes to correct genetic disorders. Nucleoside analogs mimic natural nucleotides, disrupting DNA/RNA synthesis. Ribozymes are catalytic RNA molecules that can cleave specific RNA targets, while aptamers are synthetic oligonucleotides that bind to proteins, blocking their function.</p></p>
