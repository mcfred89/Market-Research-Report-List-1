<p><p>The oral antibiotics market comprises several key categories, including Beta Lactams and Beta Lactamase Inhibitors, Quinolones, and Macrolides. Beta Lactams target bacterial cell walls, often paired with Beta Lactamase Inhibitors to counteract resistance. Quinolones are broad-spectrum agents that interfere with bacterial DNA replication, while Macrolides inhibit protein synthesis, making them effective against various infections. Together, these antibiotic classes play crucial roles in treating bacterial infections, each with unique mechanisms and applications, addressing diverse clinical needs.</p></p>
