<p><p>The oxidizing bleaching agents market is primarily divided into two types: oxidizing bleach and reducing bleach. Oxidizing bleaches, such as hydrogen peroxide and sodium hypochlorite, work by releasing oxygen or chlorine to break down color pigments, making them effective in various applications, including textiles and paper. On the other hand, reducing bleaches, like sodium bisulfite, remove color through reduction processes, often used in specific industries such as food processing and wastewater treatment. Both types play crucial roles in achieving desired whiteness and brightness in products.</p></p>
