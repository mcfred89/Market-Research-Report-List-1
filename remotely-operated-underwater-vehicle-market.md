<p><p>The Remotely Operated Underwater Vehicle (ROV) market is primarily categorized into two types: intervention and observation. Intervention ROVs are equipped for complex tasks such as maintenance, repair, and installation in underwater environments, often featuring manipulators and tools. In contrast, observation ROVs are designed for exploratory purposes, equipped with cameras and sensors to gather data and provide visual monitoring of underwater conditions without direct interaction. Both types serve crucial roles in industries like oil and gas, marine research, and environmental monitoring.</p></p>
