<p><p>The Residential Combined Heat and Power (CHP) market comprises three primary technologies: fuel cells, engines, and micro turbines. Fuel cells convert chemical energy from fuels directly into electricity, producing heat as a byproduct. Engine-based systems, typically internal combustion engines, generate electricity while utilizing waste heat for residential heating. Micro turbines are small turbines that also produce power and utilize recovered heat, offering high efficiency in compact designs. Each technology serves to enhance energy efficiency and reduce residential energy costs.</p></p>
